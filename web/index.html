Why it exists

Three frustrations, turned into three promises.

Lyra started from a short list of complaints about every other player. Each one became a thing it refuses to get wrong.

Beautiful & native

Not a theme that approximates KDE — a real Qt Quick / Kirigami app that is a Plasma app. It inherits your actual Breeze colors, accent, and fonts. Light or dark, it’s already yours.

was: ugly, dated, off-brand

Your whole library

A fast scan into a real index, full-text search, and browsing by song, album, artist, or genre — with the album art pulled from your files. Built for a collection, not a playlist.

was: slow, shallow, forgetful

The audio, done right

A lock-free real-time engine: gapless playback, ReplayGain loudness, a clean resampler, and a seek bar you can actually drag. Through PipeWire, the way modern Linux audio expects.

How it's built

“Rust brain, Qt face.”

Every line of logic is Rust — the engine, the library, the metadata. The interface is a thin QML/Kirigami skin bridged through CXX-Qt, so it looks native because it is native. A real-time audio thread pulls from a lock-free ring buffer; nothing blocks the UI, and nothing stutters the sound.

UIQML · Kirigami via CXX-Qt
CoreRust · async + RT thread
Decodesymphonia
Outputcpal → PipeWire
Loudness / DSPebur128 · biquad · rubato
LibrarySQLite + FTS5
DesktopMPRIS2 (D-Bus)
TargetKDE Plasma 6 · Wayland
